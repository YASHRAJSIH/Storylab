Labour and finance are intricately connected in the economic outlook presented by the DIW Berlin. Real wages, a significant component of labour income, are projected to rise by 3.5% in advanced economies in 2024, which is expected to support consumption even after the fiscal stimulus expires. This trend is driven by the solid labour market situation, with unemployment rates forecasted to decrease in most European countries. However, fiscal policy is expected to be slightly restrictive in most economies during the forecast period, and the debt-to-GDP ratio is projected to fall from 63.6% to 62.6%.
