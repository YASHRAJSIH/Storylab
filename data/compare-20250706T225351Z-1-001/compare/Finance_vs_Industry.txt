Finance and industry are closely interconnected in the economic context presented in the report. Fiscal policy stimuli, including those in China, have significantly contributed to global growth, with the Chinese economy growing by 5.2 percent in 2023. However, the industrial sector, which has been weak globally with a 3.9 percent contraction, is expected to gradually overcome this slump. The manufacturing industry, in particular, is likely to recover somewhat, supported by an increase in labor volume and continuous employment growth.

Despite these positive signs, there are challenges for the industrial sector. Many governments are holding back on additional spending due to fiscal deficit and debt rules, and international trade is expected to experience only moderate growth. Additionally, the manufacturing industry continues to be weak, especially in Japan and Germany.
