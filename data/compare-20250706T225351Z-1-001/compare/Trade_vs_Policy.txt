Trade and policy are intricately connected in the context of the European Union's (EU) energy sector, particularly in relation to sanctions against Russia and the EU's climate policy. The EU has the ability to control its exports of sanctioned goods and technologies to third countries, such as China, Brazil, and Vietnam, which can then re-export to Russia, mitigating the stranded assets problem (Chowdhry et al., 2024, p.10). However, the ongoing Russo-Ukrainian war and the impending expiration of the gas transit agreement between Russia and Ukraine in 2024 pose a risk of complete Russian imports to the EU, potentially leading to EU sanctions on Russian energy (Hinz et al., 2021, p.4).
